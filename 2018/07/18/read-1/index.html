Mixture-Rank Matrix Approximation for Collaborative Filtering

Motivation

  • In many real-world user-item rating matrices, submatrices with different ranks could coexist. Adopting a fixed rank for all users and items cannot perfectly model the internal structures of the rating matrix, which will lead to imperfect approximations as well as degraded recommendation accuracy.

Contribution

  • propose a mixture-rank matrix approximation (MRMA) method, in which user-item ratings are represented by a mixture of LRMA models with different ranks
  • a learning algorithm capitalizing on iterated condition modes is proposed to cope with the non-convex optimization problem associated with MRMA

Embedding Global Information in Local Matrix Approximation Models for Collaborative Filtering

Motivation

  • MA models perform poorly at detecting strong associations among a small set of items. Therefore, it is desirable to consider local associations to enhance model accuracy.
  • the prediction quality of local models based on submatrices is generally low, because
    • local models may easily overfit due to insufficient training data in clusters
    • the overall associations shared among all users/items are not captured
  • ensemble learning leads to another problem: the overall computation overhead is usually very high

Contribution

  • propose GLOMA, a scalable and accurate MA-based method which can capture both localized relationships in submatrices and global structure among all users and items
  • instead of relying on ensemble techniques, GLOMA attempts to embed a global MA model, which contains the global information, into the learning process of local MA models
  • a new clustering method is proposed, in which every user or item is characterized by its rating distribution.

Method

  • use the previously-trained model to help local models capture the global information by planting U and V into the learning process of local models

Low-Rank Matrix Approximation with Stability

Motivation

  • existing LRMA methods cannot provide stable matrix approximations，and thus have low generalization performance
  • cross validation methods have the drawback that the amount of data available for model learning is reduced
  • Ensemble LRMA methods are computationally expensive due to the training of sub-models

Contribution

  • this work first introduces the stability concept in LRMA, which can provide theoretical guidelines for deriving stable matrix approximation
  • a stable LRMA algorithm design framework is proposed, which can achieve high stability, i.e., high generalization performance by designing and solving new optimization objectives derived based on stability analysis
